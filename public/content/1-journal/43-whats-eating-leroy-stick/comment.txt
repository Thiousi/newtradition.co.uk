There’s been a good deal of attention paid to the spoof (link: http://www.twitter.com/bpglobalpr text: BP public relations) tweets at BPGlobalPR over the past few days.

To be honest, I tend towards the view that it’s a cheap shot to satirise the environmental catastrophe that has engulfed (sorry for the pun) BP — never mind the commercial crisis that it has created.

Despite being less than enamoured with the motivation of the BPGlobalPR idea, I did find the thinking of the guy behind the tweets — Leroy Stick — fascinating.

He has penned a post at (link: http://www.gizmodo.com/5553988/meet-leroy-stick--the-man-behind-bpglobalpr text: Gizmodo) (thanks to (link: http://marciikeler.com/ text: Marci Ikeler) for tweeting the link at (link:http://www.twitter.com/marciikeler text: @marciikeler) by the way) explaining what motivated him. His perspective on brands and their relationship with wider society is really interesting.
